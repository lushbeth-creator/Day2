When Underwriting Needs More
Common Reasons for Pends

A pend isn't a no, it's a request for more information. Most pends trace back to a handful of avoidable causes. Tap each card to see the fix, and remember: everything goes on the ticket, never the lead.

Common Pend

Documents Uploaded to the Lead

Supporting docs or underwriting comments were added to the lead, and the support team never got notified.
Tap for the fix
What to Do

Attach everything to the ticket that notified you of the pend, documents and notes both.

The support team does not receive notifications when attachments or comments are added to the lead, so anything uploaded there sits unseen.

Common Pend

Missing Equipment or Processing Network

One or both fields were left blank on the deal, often on VAR-only requests.
Tap for the fix
What to Do

Include both, always. Equipment and processing network must be on every deal, even if you are only requesting a VAR.

Never leave either field blank. Double-check the processing network any time a platform change is involved.

Common Pend

Platform Change After the MPA

The platform or retail-to-wholesale flip happened after the MPA went out for signature.
Tap for the fix
What to Do

Make changes before the MPA is sent or signed. Changes after the fact alter the required fields and can change the deal entirely.

Same rule for retail-to-wholesale flips: complete the flip first, then send the MPA.

Common Pend

Incomplete Owner Details or Control Prong

Owner information is missing, or the owner details and Control Prong don't match each other.
Tap for the fix
What to Do

Complete both fully and consistently. The owner's details and Control Prong must match one another.

Double-check both before submission, a mismatch is one of the fastest ways to earn a pend.
